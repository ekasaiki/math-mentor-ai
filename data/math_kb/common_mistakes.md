## Algebra

### Ignoring Domain Restrictions
Dividing by zero or taking square roots of negative numbers leads to invalid solutions.

### Losing Solutions
Squaring both sides of an equation can introduce extraneous solutions.
Always verify the final answer.

---

## Calculus

### Incorrect Application of Power Rule
For f(x) = x^n, forgetting to multiply by n is a common error.

### Chain Rule Errors
Failing to differentiate the inner function correctly.

### Ignoring Continuity in Limits
Direct substitution is not valid if the function is discontinuous.

---

## Probability

### Assuming Independence
Events are often assumed independent without justification.

### Incorrect Sample Space
Not listing all possible outcomes leads to wrong probabilities.
